About

Why ninety three

Project 93 for Justice is a Canterbury Christ Church University project using the arts to open up conversations about social justice - and to leave behind resources other people can pick up and use.

Placeholder copy. This page is laid out and ready - the client's own words about the project's history and people drop straight in.

This is where the project explains itself: where the name came from, who started it, and what it is trying to do. It is the page people reach when they have seen something they liked and want to know who is behind it.

What we do

We run campaigns around a social issue, put on activities that bring people into the argument, and collect the films, books and music that made the argument well in the first place. The resource collection is the part that outlives any single campaign.

Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.

Who is involved

Staff, students and interns across the university, plus the partners who work with us on individual campaigns. Names, roles and photographs go here.
