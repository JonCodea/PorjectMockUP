Background texture options

The brief asks for no flat solid colour - the backgrounds should feel like rock / stone, with cracks and grain. Texture lies in a static mockup, so here are four treatments rendered at real size in a real browser. Scroll through and react to them.

All four are generated in code (SVG filters) rather than photographs: nothing to license, nothing to download, scales to any screen, and weighs virtually nothing. Use the buttons at the bottom to jump between them, toggle the text scrim, and hide these notes for a clean look.

The headline, body copy and orange button are identical in each so you're judging only the background.

Fine grain

What it is: black with a barely-there film grain over it. Kills the "flat digital black" feel without announcing itself.

Pro: the safest, most premium option. Text stays perfectly legible. Never looks dated or cheap.

Con: it may read as too subtle against the brief - it is not literally "rock".

Stone slab

What it is: a lit stone surface - real mottled relief, like a slab of dark rock catching a low light.

Pro: unmistakably the "rock" the brief asks for. Closest to the literal reading.

Con: the busiest, and the one that can tip into "cheap stock texture" if pushed any further. Toggle the scrim off - text stays readable at this strength, so the scrim is here for depth and focus rather than rescue. It stops being optional if we lighten the stone.

Cracked stone

What it is: the stone slab, plus a real crack system - drawn as lines, then warped so they fracture organically rather than looking like a vector doodle.

Pro: exactly the "cracks" the brief asks for. The cracks sit away from the text, on the empty side - that's the trick that keeps it usable.

Con: the most literal. Risks feeling thematic-with-a-capital-T on every page. Best used on the Home hero only.

Layered editorial

What it is: six layers - black base, slow stone mottling, a warm orange bloom, cracks kept to the edges, fine grain, then a vignette.

Why this one: it is textured stone in every direction the brief asks for, but it stays a background. The orange bloom is the bit that makes it feel designed for Project 93 rather than a texture bought off a shelf.

Scales: dial the crack layer up for the Home hero, off for content pages - same system, one variable.
